Police

Main Division Squads

The main division squads of the Royal Bhutan Police work together to maintain law and order, protect citizens, and respond to emergencies. These include the Traffic Police, Crime Investigation Division, Special Reserve Police, Communication Division, Training Division, Medical Unit, and Rescue and Disaster Response teams, each responsible for public safety, investigations, emergency response, and community protection.

Brigadier Phub Gyaltshen

Brigadier Phub Gyaltshen is the Chief of Police of the Royal Bhutan Police (RBP). He was graciously appointed to this position by His Majesty The King on February 15, 2026, succeeding to the highest law enforcement role in the country.

Rescue

Police in Bhutan help rescue people during accidents, crimes, and emergencies. They respond quickly to road accidents, disasters, and dangerous situations by providing protection, controlling the scene, helping evacuation, and working with ambulance, fire, and rescue teams to save lives and ensure public safety.

Record

The Royal Bhutan Police (Royal Bhutan Police) has a strong record of helping people during emergencies and disasters in Bhutan. They assist in road accident rescues, disaster response, crowd control during emergencies, and coordination with ambulance, fire, and army teams. During floods, landslides, and other crises, police officers help with evacuation, rescue operations, and maintaining public safety, earning trust for their quick response and service to the nation.

Firefighter

Main Division Squads

The main division squads of firefighters in Bhutan include fire rescue squads, emergency response squads, search-and-rescue teams, disaster response units, and fire prevention teams. These squads work together to control fires, rescue people, provide emergency assistance, and protect lives and property during disasters and emergencies.

Brigadier Phub Gyaltshen

Brigadier Phub Gyaltshen is the Chief of Police of the Royal Bhutan Police (RBP). He was graciously appointed to this position by His Majesty The King on February 15, 2026, succeeding to the highest law enforcement role in the country.

Rescue

Firefighters in Bhutan rescue people during fires, road accidents, floods, and other emergencies. They help evacuate people from dangerous situations, control fires, provide emergency assistance, and protect lives and property. Firefighters work with police, ambulance teams, and rescue agencies to respond quickly during disasters and save lives.

Record

Firefighters in Bhutan have a strong record of helping people during fires, accidents, and emergencies. They respond quickly to rescue people from burning buildings, road accidents, floods, and other dangerous situations while protecting lives and property. Their bravery, quick response, and dedication have helped save many lives across the country.

Desuung (Guardians of Peace)

Main Volunteer Division

The main volunteer divisions of Desuung include disaster response teams, community service volunteers, health and safety support teams, traffic management volunteers, and emergency relief squads. These volunteer divisions help during disasters, public events, emergencies, and national service activities across Bhutan.

About Leadership

Administered via the Central Command Office. Regulates large-scale civic support, volunteer assignments, and community integration plans.

Rescue

The Desuung plays an important role in rescuing and helping people during emergencies and natural disasters in Bhutan. Desuups assist during floods, landslides, fires, road accidents, and health emergencies by supporting rescue operations, providing relief materials, managing evacuation, and helping local communities. Their dedication, discipline, and volunteer service have helped save many lives across the country.

Record

The Desuung has a strong record of helping people during disasters, emergencies, and national crises in Bhutan. Desuups have assisted in flood and landslide rescue operations, disaster relief distribution, road safety management, and community support programs. During the COVID-19 pandemic, they played a major role in quarantine monitoring, delivery of essential supplies, and public safety services, earning great respect for their dedication and volunteer service to the nation.

Ambulance Services

Emergency Medical Services

Ambulance-based Emergency Medical Services in Bhutan provide quick medical transportation and emergency care during accidents, illnesses, and disasters. Operated under the Ministry of Health, ambulances help transport patients safely to hospitals while providing first aid and urgent medical support, especially in remote and emergency situations.

Lyonpo Tandin Wangchuk

His Excellency Tandin Wangchuk was reappointed as Bhutan's Health Minister in January 2024, marking his second tenure in this pivotal role. During his first term as Health Minister from 2013 to 2017, he led transformative initiatives that strengthened Bhutan's health system and expanded access to essential services.

Rescue

Ambulance services in Bhutan rescue people by providing quick medical care and transportation during accidents, illnesses, and disasters. Ambulance teams give first aid and safely transport patients to hospitals, especially in remote and emergency situations.

Record

Ambulance services in Bhutan have a strong record of helping people during medical emergencies, accidents, and natural disasters. Ambulance teams provide quick first aid, emergency treatment, and safe transportation to hospitals, helping save many lives, especially in remote areas and during floods, landslides, and road accidents.

Army / Armed Forces

Main Division Squads

The Royal Bhutan Army has several main division squads that work together to protect the country and help people during emergencies. These include the Infantry, Engineering, Medical, Communication, Logistics, Training, and Rescue squads, each responsible for defense, construction, healthcare, communication, supplies, soldier training, and disaster response.

Lt. General Batoo Tshering

Goongloen Gongma (Dzongkha for 'Senior General') Batoo Tshering, DYG, DRT, DW, DT, DK, is the current Chief Operations Officer of the Royal Bhutan Army (COO). He succeeded the outgoing chief, Goongloen Gongma Lam Dorji on 1 November 2005.

Rescue

The Royal Bhutan Army plays a major role in disaster response and rescue operations across Bhutan. Their rescue teams assist people during floods, landslides, earthquakes, fires, road accidents, and emergency evacuations, especially in remote mountain regions. The army is well known for providing emergency rescue and evacuation, disaster relief distribution, medical assistance, search-and-rescue missions, and continuous support to local communities during natural disasters and emergencies.

Record

The Royal Bhutan Army has a strong record of helping people during disasters and emergencies across Bhutan. Their rescue teams respond quickly to floods, landslides, fires, road accidents, and medical emergencies, especially in remote areas. In 2020, four RBA soldiers sacrificed their lives while rescuing civilians during severe flash floods in Gelephu. The army also provides emergency evacuation, disaster relief, medical support, and search-and-rescue operations, earning deep respect for their courage and dedication to the nation
